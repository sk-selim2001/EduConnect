Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about our counselling services

How do I prepare for my first counselling session?

We recommend preparing by thinking about your academic history, current challenges, and future goals. Bring any relevant transcripts, test scores, or documents. It's also helpful to write down specific questions you want to address during the session.

What's the difference between academic and career counselling?

Academic counselling focuses on course selection, study strategies, and academic planning to help you succeed in your current educational program. Career counselling helps you explore career options, identify your strengths and interests, and develop a plan to achieve your professional goals.

Can I change counselors if I'm not satisfied?

Absolutely. We want you to feel comfortable with your counselor. If after your first session you feel another counselor might be a better fit, simply contact our support team and we'll help you find a better match at no additional cost.

How often should I schedule counselling sessions?

This depends on your individual needs. Many students start with weekly or bi-weekly sessions, then transition to monthly check-ins. Your counselor will work with you to determine the optimal schedule based on your goals and progress.
